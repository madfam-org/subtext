The gap in your stack

Transcripts tell you what was said.
They don't tell you what was meant.

ASR gets you words. Sentiment APIs give you a 0-to-1 smile score. Neither captures the moment a deal tips, a customer gives up, or the conversation found its rhythm. That's the subtext — and until now, it lived only in the head of whoever was on the call.

Did the customer feel heard?

A transcript can't tell you if your agent validated the concern — or talked past it. Subtext detects acknowledgment density and emotional alignment in real time.

Was the sales rep in rhythm?

Pace matching is the single strongest correlate of a closed deal. We surface it as a live signal — before the call ends, not in a post-mortem.

Is the caller about to churn?

Frustration looks different from sadness, which looks different from resignation. We separate the three, and weight them against what the caller came in with.

Three steps to live signals

Ship it this afternoon.

Step 01

Open a session

One REST call per conversation. You get a session_id and a WebSocket URL scoped to it. Sessions are cheap — open per-call, per-ticket, per-meeting.

curl -X POST https://api.subtext.live/api/v1/sessions \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $TOKEN" \
  -d '{"context":"sales_call","locale":"en-US"}'

// → { "session_id": "sx_01HX…", "ws_url": "wss://…" }
Step 02

Stream audio or transcript

Connect to the WebSocket. Push audio frames or live transcript segments. Your client doesn't need to batch, chunk, or pre-process — Subtext handles the rest.

const ws = new WebSocket(session.ws_url);

ws.send(JSON.stringify({
  type: "transcript",
  speaker: "agent",
  text: "I hear you — let me take care of that.",
  t: 1776600000.12
}));
Step 03

React to signals

Signal frames stream back continuously. Surface them to your agent coach, your supervisor UI, your risk scorer, your analytics pipeline. You decide what "subtext" means to your app.

ws.onmessage = (e) => {
  const signal = JSON.parse(e.data);
  // { type: "signal", name: "rapport", value: 0.82, delta: +0.09 }
  if (signal.name === "frustration_spike") {
    coach.alertSupervisor(signal);
  }
};
